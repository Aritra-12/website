My Problems

Other Notable Papers

A collection of article and books I find very useful and interesting. They range from olympiad-level handouts to more complicated and abstract concepts. I hope you find these referrences useful and enjoy reading them.

Problems From The Book – Titu Andreescu, and Gabriel Dospinescu; PFTB is great book! It covers several ideas and concepts, at am IMO level (and further!) while presenting challanging problems and clear explanations. I really enjoyed this book!

An Infinitely Large Napkin – Evan Chen; Perhaps one of the most ambitious and complex olympiad handouts, Evan Chen's Napkin is a must-read. It is a constantly developing project, aimed at making higher mathematics accessible to high school students.

Combinatorial Number Theory – Henry Liu; This is an article I personally really enjoyed reading. It proves and exemplifies various very important and interesting theorems and it ends with a collection of training problems.

Olympiad Combinatorics – Pranav A. Sriram; Olympiad Combinatorics is probably the most popular and suitable book discussing IMO-level combinatorics. I found it very well written and remarkably useful.

Number Theory: Concepts and Problems – Titu Andreescu, Gabriel Dospinescu, and Oleg Mushkarov; I am very passionate about number theory, and despite reading many books, this was my all-time favourite. It's great book, explaining various concepts ranging from olympiad-level theorems to higher mathematics and has great problems!

Problems in Real Analysis – Teodora-Liliana T. Rădulescu, Vicențiu D. Rădulescu, and Titu Andreescu; Real analysis is an important tool and this book is a great way to learn it and simultaneously see how to use it in olympiad problems.
